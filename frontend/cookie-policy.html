What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They allow websites to remember information about your visit — such as your login state or preferences. Similar technologies include web beacons, pixels, and local storage.

Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for StemScriber to function and cannot be disabled without breaking core features:

  • Session authentication cookies — keep you logged in as you navigate
  • CSRF protection cookies — prevent cross-site request forgery attacks
  • Cloudflare security cookies — manage load balancing and DDoS protection
  • JWT tokens — secure session management

These are set automatically and do not require your consent.

Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance your experience by remembering your preferences:

  • User preference cookies — remember your settings
  • Theme preferences — store your UI customization choices

Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how users interact with StemScriber — page views, feature usage, and performance. These require your consent before being set.

Third-Party Cookies

Third-party services we integrate with may set their own cookies:

  • Cloudflare — security, DDoS protection, and CDN functionality
  • Stripe — payment processing and fraud prevention
  • Google — OAuth authentication and identity verification

Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser's settings:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies and other site data

Disabling strictly necessary cookies will prevent you from logging in and using StemScriber. Disabling functional or analytics cookies will not affect core functionality.

Consent

Strictly necessary cookies are set automatically without requiring consent, as they are essential for the service to function. Non-essential cookies (functional, analytics, certain third-party) require your explicit consent before being set.
